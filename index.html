How it all began...

A quick glance at my life.

I was born in 1987 in Austria and had a fascination with technology from an early age, but we didn't have the money to buy a computer for me. I still remember when I got a cheap V-Tech computer, which I took apart to see how it worked.

In elementary school, I was always a good pupil and had good grades. I struggled with German and geography, but I was able to overcome those difficulties. I spent a lot of time hiking and crafting with my grandpa, who was a carpenter, which was a lot of fun.

When I was 10 years old, I went to high school. Due to my laziness, I had bad grades and had to switch to secondary school, where my grades greatly improved, and I successfully finished school.

After I finished secondary school at 15, I began an apprenticeship as a car body worker, where I also learned the basics of electronics. Unfortunately, I could not finish the apprenticeship because the company closed.

After my apprenticeship was cut short, I took a year off from working and started my journey into the computer scene. A friend of mine taught me Linux and hacking, and he pushed me in the right direction so I could learn to use Google to find information on my own.

I celebrated my first successful WiFi hacks and continued my journey in Linux administration by deploying my own web server. I was also very active in the download scene at that time. This continued for a couple of years until I got into Arduino, Raspberry Pi, and other microcontrollers for home automation and hacking tools.
